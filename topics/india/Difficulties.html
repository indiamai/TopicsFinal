Difficulties

There are still problems with the methods we have discussed. For example, the BRDF model requires complex computations, meaning it cannot be implemented sufficiently in real-time. Simplifications of the model to avoid these calculations lose generality, or require large amounts of input information, as is the case with the Wave-BSDF.

We also have some issues with rendering natural structures vs man-made ones. It is likely more efficient to represent a man-made structure computationally, as it may be repetitive and/or predictable in a way that can be easily represented by a function. Natural structures, however, are much less predictable. They can be modelled by randomly distributed functions, but this wonâ€™t be able to precisely represent any given natural structure. A given natural structure could be precisely represented by giving input describing the surface, however the difficulty is then in obtaining this information, which most likely will require some specialist equipment.
