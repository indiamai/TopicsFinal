Spectral Rendering

When you deal with colours in terms of the wavelength of the constituent light, the common method for rendering colour, RGB, is no longer accurate, as you must individually convert each wavelength to a corresponding RGB value.

When diffraction occurs, Light is spread out into its constituent wavelengths, creating colour. Therefore, here it makes little sense to use a RGB rendering method, as each constituent part of the full spectrum must be converted into a RGB value. In cases like this, it is useful to use Spectral Rendering.

Spectral Rendering uses the actual wavelength to render the colours, which makes it significantly slower than RGB methods as there are more factors to consider.

There are some situations where RGB methods are better, for example in simply rendering the colours of material, however, since we are considering diffraction, spectral rendering is essential.
